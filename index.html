About my Experience

User Research

Tools used: Zoom, Google Suite

Each case always beings and ends with the most important factor in mind: the user.

Using information and data from our stakeholders, we drafted our proto-personas to serve as a beginning hypothesis. More often than not, we found our assumptions challenged or debunked upon our actual user interviews and tests being conducted.

Despite the challenges of finding, interviewing and testing users all via online networking and Zoom calls during the shelter-in-place orders of 2020, me and my teams always went above and beyond in finding and learning from users about their actual needs.

On top of our interviews and tests, me and my teams built targeted online surveys via Google Forms and visualized our data in Miro into core points to address in our cases.

Define and Conceptualize

Tools used: Zoom, Google Suite, Miro, Figma

Using core data points from our interviews and research, we then defined and conceptualized our users' experiences, pain points, needs and wants. All of these together create the road map that leads to a story with one end goal in mind: getting the user both what they need and want, without compromise.

The user's story is not to be a means to support a designer's pre-conceived vision. Rather, it is the users that know and determine the final product's destiny as that is whom the product is intended for. It is these stories that bring light not only to solutions for our users problems, but also to new possibilities for workflows that we didn't even know could be possible.
